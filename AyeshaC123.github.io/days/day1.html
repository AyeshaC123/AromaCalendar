Understanding Stress and Its Impact

Stress is a common response to challenging or threatening situations, and it activates the body's "fight or flight" response. While short-term stress can be beneficial for dealing with immediate threats, chronic stress can have detrimental effects on both physical and mental health. It can stem from various sources such as work pressures, relationship issues, financial difficulties, or health concerns.

The body's reaction to stress includes the release of hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which can increase heart rate, elevate blood pressure, and boost energy levels. Managing stress effectively through techniques like exercise, relaxation, proper sleep, and seeking social support is crucial for maintaining overall well-being.
